# Recommended LLVM & Compiler Textbooks

## LLVM Specific Books
- **Learn LLVM 17 (or latest version)** by Kai Nacke
  - A hands-on guide to learning LLVM by building a compiler frontend.
- **Getting Started with LLVM Core Libraries** by Bruno Cardoso Lopes and Rafael Auler
  - Provides a deep dive into the LLVM infrastructure, including the IR, Bitcode, and backend.
- **LLVM Essentials** by Suyog Sarda and Mayur Pandey
  - A practical, project-oriented guide to learning LLVM.

## General Compiler Theory
- **Engineering a Compiler** by Keith Cooper and Linda Torczon
  - Excellent for understanding the algorithms used in the middle-end and backend.
- **Compilers: Principles, Techniques, and Tools (The Dragon Book)** by Aho, Lam, Sethi, and Ullman
  - The classic textbook for frontend analysis and general compiler theory.

## Online Resources & Tutorials
- **LLVM Kaleidoscope Tutorial** (Official Docs)
  - The best starting point for building your first language with LLVM.
- **The Architecture of Open Source Applications: LLVM** by Chris Lattner
  - A high-level overview of the design philosophy behind LLVM.
- **Mapping High-Level Constructs to LLVM IR**
  - An essential community-maintained guide for seeing how C++ features map to IR.
